Use Case

Federated Learning Across European Hospitals

  • 15 Jan 2026
  • By Fed-BioMed Team

Context

A consortium of five European hospitals needed to collaborate on training a predictive model for early cancer detection. Each institution held valuable patient data but could not share it due to GDPR and internal governance policies.

Challenge

Traditional centralized approaches were not viable. Moving raw patient data across institutional boundaries posed legal, ethical, and technical barriers that could not be overcome within the project timeline.

Solution with Fed-BioMed

Fed-BioMed was deployed across all five sites. Each hospital trained the model locally on its own data. Only the aggregated model updates — never the raw data — were exchanged with the central aggregator.

Key aspects of the deployment:

  • Verified execution ensured that only approved training scripts ran on institutional infrastructure
  • Governance workflows allowed each hospital to review and approve each experiment before execution
  • Secure aggregation prevented any single party from reconstructing individual contributions

Results

The federated model achieved comparable performance to a hypothetical centralized model, while keeping all patient data within each institution. The project demonstrated that high-quality medical AI is achievable without compromising institutional responsibility.

Institutions Involved

  • Hospital A — Paris, France
  • Hospital B — Milan, Italy
  • Hospital C — Barcelona, Spain
  • Hospital D — Amsterdam, Netherlands
  • Hospital E — Berlin, Germany
