Federated Discovery

What is Federated Discovery?

Federated discovery enables users to explore what data exists across multiple institutions without accessing the raw data itself. Instead of moving sensitive datasets, institutions can expose shared metadata that helps identify available cohorts, modalities, or resources. Queries are performed across the federation while data remains locally protected. This supports study preparation and collaboration while preserving privacy.
