The Project

Challenges in healthcare

Healthcare institutions operate under strict responsibility.

  • Patient data must remain protected at all times.
  • Legal and ethical obligations limit data sharing.
  • IT systems and infrastructures vary across institutions.
  • Research collaboration requires clear governance and accountability.

At the same time, high-quality medical research increasingly depends on data from multiple institutions.

Balancing collaboration and responsibility remains a central challenge.

What is Fed-BioMed?

Fed-BioMed is an open-source framework designed to support collaborative learning in healthcare environments.

It enables hospitals and research teams to conduct federated learning experiments while ensuring that:

  • Patient data remains within each institution
  • Participation is explicitly controlled and approved
  • Research activities are transparent and traceable

The framework is specifically developed for real-world healthcare settings, where governance, security, and operational constraints are essential.

Developement pillars

Fed-BioMed is structured around four core principles.

Security

Security mechanisms are integrated into the framework to protect communications, models, and data exchanges. The goal is to support collaboration without weakening institutional safeguards.

Governance

Each participating institution retains authority over its involvement. Experiments can be approved, reviewed, monitored, and interrupted according to local policies and responsibilities.

Usability

The framework is designed for practical use in research environments with a simple graphical interface. It supports interactive experimentation while remaining compatible with common machine learning tools.

Community-driven

Fed-BioMed is developed as an open-source initiative in collaboration with healthcare and research partners. Its evolution is guided by real deployment experience and institutional needs.
